     3 +This pack is designed around a domain where I tend to be particularly strong: software engineering guidance, cod

    13 +- `RFC 2119 SHOULD`
    14 +
    15 +Good example queries:
    16 +- "How should configuration and dependencies be handled?"
    17 +- "What guidance applies to breaking API changes?"
    18 +- "What do the cited sources say about readability and naming?"
    19 +
    20 +Framework adaptation notes:
    21 +- Citation objects would be stronger than plain citation strings.
    22 +  Ideal fields: `source_id`, `section`, `version`, `ecosystem`, `normative_strength`, `url`.
    23 +- Source conflict handling would help when standards disagree.
    24 +  Engineering guidance often mixes style advice, operational practice, and normative specification language.
    25 +- A first-class distinction between requirements and recommendations would improve synthesis quality.
    26 +  `RFC 2119 MUST` and `RFC 2119 SHOULD` are not equivalent, and the framework currently treats citations as flat
         peers.
    27 +- Ranked or weighted source metadata would help.
    28 +  For this domain, an authoritative language spec or standard should usually outrank a style guide.
    29 +- Link-aware outputs would make this pack more useful in practice.
    30 +  Engineering users often want a source label plus a direct URL or section reference for follow-up.
